[[include inc:command
|picture=TIMESROWPLUS.GIF
|summary=Adds a multiple of one row of a matrix to another.
|syntax=*row+(//factor//,//matrix//,//row1//,//row2//)
|location=Press:
# MATRX (on a TI-83) or 2nd MATRX (TI-83+ or higher) to access the matrix menu.
# RIGHT to access the MATH submenu.
# ALPHA F to select *row+(, or use arrows and ENTER.
|compatibility=TI-83/84/+/SE
|size=1 byte
]]

The *row+( adds a multiple of one row to another row and returns the result. It is an elementary row operation used in Gaussian Elimination. 

[[code]]
[[1,2][3,4]]
	[[1 2]
	 [3 4]]
*row+(10,Ans,1,2)
	[[3  4 ]
	 [31 42]]
[[/code]]

+ Advanced Uses

You can add columns instead of rows with the aid of the ^^T^^ ([[[transpose]]]) command.

+ Error Conditions

* **[[[errors#invaliddim|ERR:INVALID DIM]]]** is thrown if one of the row arguments isn't a valid row (larger than the matrix size, or otherwise bad)
