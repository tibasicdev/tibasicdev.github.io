|compatibility=TI-83/84/+/SE

The *row( command multiplies a row of a matrix by a scalar factor and returns the result. It is an elementary row operation used in Gaussian Elimination. 

[[code]]
[[1,2][3,4]]
	[[1 2]
	 [3 4]]
*row(10,Ans,1)
	[[10 20]
	 [3  4 ]]
[[/code]]

+ Advanced Uses

You can multiply columns instead of rows with the aid of the ^^T^^ ([[[transpose]]]) command.

+ Error Conditions

* **[[[errors#invaliddim|ERR:INVALID DIM]]]** is thrown if the row argument isn't a valid row (is larger than the size of the matrix, or otherwise bad)
