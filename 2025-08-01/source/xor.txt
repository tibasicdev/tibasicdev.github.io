The third and final [[[binandhex|binary]]] operator is a little trickier, but it has the coolest name.  **xor** takes two numbers of expressions and checks to see if //exactly one// is True.  If both are True or both are False, it returns 0.
[[code]]1 xor 0
           1

:2 xor (3 xor 0)    (after evaluating 3 xor 0, it simplifies into True xor True.)
           0

:0 xor (1-1)^2
           0[[/code]]
